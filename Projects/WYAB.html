Interactive Product Design

What Are You Breathing?

An empathetic air pollution simulation that aims to bring an acute awareness to individuals about how both outdoor and indoor air pollution pervades every aspect of society.

Misinformation is becoming a massive problem on the internet, and is dividing societies on important issues. Designers can help general audiences gain better understanding of these critical challenges through engaging novel technologies.

My Role:

Market Analysis & User Research

I compiled several reviews from similar mobile apps and analysed customer needs and common pain points. Two separate questionnaires were also distributed to gain an understanding on how people viewed air pollution.

Ideation, Rapid Prototyping & User Testing

I was involved extensively in the entire process of shaping the webapp, from establishing the idea based on our research, to sketching and prototyping on Figma. We employed several methods in our user testing, such as the system usability scale and informal interviews. The insights gained were synthesised and applied into further iterations.

Unity Scene Construction

All scenes in the webapp were constructed by myself with help from the Unity Asset store and my partner. This includes several of the animations within the scene, logos and backgrounds, as well as the layout of the interface.

The Problem

The issue of air pollution misinformation is largely ignored due to people’s misconception of it as a non-urgent problem that won’t affect their health and the environment. As the Internet has now become a major part of our everyday digital lives - and is not something kept separate or apart - individuals can’t distinguish between what is factual and how this global issue in fact directly affects everyone including the environment.

Our Solution

We conceptualised and created a web application focused on providing qualitative information in the form of a city simulation, where the air pollution is visualised. As the user interacts with the application, they can understand how air pollution affects countries of varying economic statuses and populations, and gain an informed understanding on the topic.
